ACTION MOVIES

RUSH HOUR

Directed by Brett Ratner

           Rush Hour is a television series based on the popular film franchise of the same name. The show premiered in 2016 and follows the adventures of Detective Lee, played by Jon Foo, and Detective Carter, played by Justin Hires, as they fight crime and solve cases in Los Angeles. The show is a blend of action, comedy, and drama, with each episode featuring high-octane car chases, intense fight scenes, and hilarious banter between the two leads. Lee, a Hong Kong police officer, and Carter, a detective from the LAPD, come from vastly different backgrounds, but their mutual respect and complementary skills make them a dynamic duo.
























300 (2006)

Directed by Zack Synder

           300 is a 2006 epic action film directed by Zack Snyder, based on the comic book series of the same name by Frank Miller and Lynn Varley. The movie tells the story of the Battle of Thermopylae, in which 300 Spartan warriors, led by their king Leonidas, fought to defend their homeland against a massive invading Persian army. The film is known for its stylized visuals, with many scenes shot against a green screen and enhanced with computer-generated imagery. The use of slow-motion and high-contrast visuals gives the movie a unique, graphic novel-like feel. The film's soundtrack, which features a mix of orchestral music and hard rock, adds to the intense, visceral experience.

COMEDY MOVIES

21 Jump Street (2012)

Directed by Phil Lord, Chris Miller

           21 Jump Street is a 2012 American action-comedy film directed by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, and it is based on the popular television series of the same name. The movie stars Jonah Hill and Channing Tatum as two underachieving police officers who are sent undercover to infiltrate a high school and stop a dangerous drug ring. The film is known for its self-aware humor and clever subversion of the tropes and cliches of the high school and buddy-cop genres. Hill and Tatum's chemistry is a highlight of the movie, as they play off each other's strengths and weaknesses to hilarious effect. The film's supporting cast, including Dave Franco, Brie Larson, and Ice Cube, also deliver strong performances.

































Central Intelligence (2016)

Directed by Rawson Marshall Thurber

           Central Intelligence is a 2016 American action-comedy film directed by Rawson Marshall Thurber, and it stars Dwayne Johnson and Kevin Hart in the lead roles. The movie follows Calvin Joyner, a mild-mannered accountant, who reconnects with an old high school friend, Bob Stone, who is now a rogue CIA agent.Central Intelligence is notable for its twists and turns, with the plot taking unexpected and often outrageous turns that keep the audience on their toes. Despite being a comedy, Central Intelligence also touches on themes of identity, self-acceptance, and the dangers of living up to societal expectations. The movie explores the idea that true strength comes from embracing one's unique qualities, rather than conforming to what others think is acceptable.

KOREAN DRAMA

Parasite (2019)

Directed by Bong Joon-ho

           Parasite is a 2019 South Korean black comedy thriller film directed by Bong Joon-ho. The film follows the Kim family, who are struggling to make ends meet in a small semi-basement apartment in a low-income area of Seoul. The son, Ki-woo, lands a job as a tutor for the wealthy Park family's daughter, Da-hye. The Kims see an opportunity to improve their situation, and slowly insinuate themselves into the lives of the Parks, posing as highly qualified professionals. As the Kims' scheme becomes more elaborate, the film explores themes of social class, greed, and morality. However, their plans are disrupted when a former housekeeper returns to the Park household, revealing a shocking secret about the basement. The film takes a darker turn as the Kims' deception is exposed and they find themselves entangled in a violent struggle for survival.

























Miracle in Cell No.7 (2013)

Directed by Lee Hwan-kyung

           Miracle in Cell No.7 is a 2013 South Korean drama film directed by Lee Hwan-kyung. The movie tells the heartwarming story of a father and his daughter, and their unwavering love for each other in the face of unimaginable adversity. The film centers around the character of Lee Yong-gu, a mentally challenged man who is wrongfully accused of a crime he did not commit and sentenced to death. In prison, he is placed in cell number seven, where he forms a close bond with his cellmates. One day, Yong-gu's young daughter, Ye-seung, comes to visit him in prison, but is denied entry. Determined to see his daughter, Yong-gu devises a plan with his cellmates to smuggle her into the prison. Ye-seung's innocent presence in the prison brings joy to the other inmates and eventually leads to a series of events that bring the father and daughter closer together.

Train to Busan(2016)

Directed by Yeon Sang-ho

           Train to Busan is a 2016 South Korean horror-thriller film directed by Yeon Sang-ho. The movie tells the story of a group of passengers who become trapped on a train during a zombie outbreak, and their fight for survival as they make their way to the city of Busan. The film follows the character of Seok-woo, a workaholic fund manager who reluctantly takes his young daughter, Soo-an, on a train ride to Busan to visit her mother. As the train departs, a mysterious virus breaks out in South Korea, turning people into violent zombies. One infected woman boards the train, and chaos ensues as the virus rapidly spreads among the passengers. Seok-woo and the other survivors must band together to fend off the zombie attacks and find a way to escape the train and reach safety. Along the way, they face numerous obstacles and must make difficult choices as they struggle to survive and protect each other.

FANSTASY MOVIES

Bridge to Terabithia (2007)

Directed by Gábor Csupó

           Bridge to Terabithia is a 2007 American fantasy drama film directed by Gabor Csupo and adapted from Katherine Paterson's award-winning novel of the same name. The film follows the story of two young friends, Jesse Aarons and Leslie Burke, who create an imaginary world called Terabithia to escape their mundane lives. Set in rural Virginia, the film centers around the unlikely friendship between Jesse, a shy and artistic boy who dreams of becoming a professional artist, and Leslie, a new girl in town who is smart, outgoing and imaginative. Together, they build a secret hideaway in the woods near their homes, which they name Terabithia. There, they create a magical world filled with mythical creatures, where they reign as king and queen.





























Clash of the Titans (2010)

Directed by Louis Leterrier

           The film follows the story of Perseus, a demigod and son of Zeus, who must embark on a perilous journey to save the city of Argos from destruction by the vengeful god, Hades. Along the way, he must battle a series of terrifying monsters, including the deadly Medusa and the fearsome Kraken, in order to fulfill his destiny and save the city. The film is notable for its stunning visual effects and action-packed fight scenes, which bring the mythical world of ancient Greece to life on the big screen. It also explores themes of destiny, free will, and the balance between gods and mortals.
